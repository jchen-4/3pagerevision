One popular example can be found commonly throughout New York City subway stations. The wood blocks are seemingly there to allow for the separation of specific seats, but are actually there to make it difficult for the homeless to sleep there.
Hostile architecture is not always extremely obvious. There are instances, like this bench in Los Angeles, where things are designed with a certain aesthetic, distracting you from its true purpose.
However, hostile architecture can also be blatantly obvious. This overpass in Guangzhou, China has literal spikes under it to prevent homeless people from taking shelter.
